Bulgaria

History

The land that gave birth to the legendary Orpheus and Spartacus, Bulgaria is a country with a long, tumultuous and fascinating history. It has been invaded, conquered and settled by Greeks, Scythians, Romans, Byzantines and Turks, all of whom left their indelible marks on the landscape. Bulgaria’s medieval ‘Golden Age’, when the Bulgar Khans ruled over one of the largest empires in Europe, was bright but brief, while 500 years of subsequent, brutal Turkish domination isolated the country from the rest of Europe. More recently, Bulgaria spent four decades as a totalitarian Soviet satellite, again leaving this small Balkan nation in the shadows as far as the Western world was concerned.In the last years of the 20th century Bulgaria began opening up, and is one of the newest members of the EU.

Landmarks

The long past of Bulgaria has left some remarkable sights and landmarks. A great example is Tsarevets - a fortress located on a hill in Veliko Tarnovo in northern Bulgaria. It was the primary fortress of the Second Bulgarian Empire when Veliko Tarnovo was a capital. One of the oldest cities in Bulgaria is Plodviv, which attracts tourists from all over the world with it's ancient but at the same time modern look. The city is based on seven hills, some of which are 250 metres high. Plovdiv has over 200 well preserved archeological sites, for instance The Ancient Theatre which is situated between two of the Three hills (on which the city was built). Plovdiv has the longest pedestrian zone in Europe with total legnth of 1750m.

Traditions

A lot of traditions have been preserved during the Turkish domination and they are still observed in the present. Bulgaria is well-known around the world with it's folk dances. They are performed with traditinal dress and music. Bulgarian folk dances are line dances, with the dancers holding hands in a straight or curved line, facing in toward the center of the dance space. Moreover, in Bulgaria there is a equivallent to Halloween, a ritual to scare away evil spirits. The ritual is performed by men, called Kukeri, dressed with costumes, which include masks of animals, as well as bell rings attached to the belt. A prominent annual traditional for health is performed every year on 7th of January, when a priest throws a cross in the water of a river and the one who gets it the first is going to be healthy through the following year.

Cuisine

Bulgarian cuisine shares characteristics with other Balkans cuisines and it has been influenced by the Turkish cuisine. The Bulgarian food is well known for it's diversity. For instance, it features a variety of soups, such as the cold soup Tarator or Skembe chorba -spicy soup made of trip, reputed in Bulgaria to be a "hangover cure". Moreover, Banitsa is one of the most popular and consumed breakfast in Bulgaria. It's a pastry of eggs, white cheese and yogurt between phyllolayers. Main courses are typically water-based stews, either vegetarian or with meat(Pork and lamb are common,but chicken is widely used too).In addtion, grilling is promenint way for cooking in Bulgaria and is called skara. The most famous main grilled dishes are Kyufte(meatballs of minced pork meat, seasoned with traditional spices and shaped in a flattened ball) and Kebapche (similar to meatballs, but seasoned with cumin and shaped in a stick). To conclude, Bulgarian sweets are simple but delicious. Pupmkin dessert and Kozunak(sweet leavened bread usually prepared for Easter) are good examples.
